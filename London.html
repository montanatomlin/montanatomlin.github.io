Weekend in London

Friday Afternoon

Sighseeing In London

Firt stop was the Buckingham palace.Buckingham Palace is the London home and the administrative center of the British royal family. The enormous building and extensive gardens are an important site of ceremonial and political affairs in the United Kingdom, as well as a major tourist attraction.Lastly, the Westminister Abbey. It is probably the most recognised and famous churches on the planet. It is built on a site that has served as a place of worship for about 14 centuries.It has played host to royal weddings including that of Queen Elizabeth II and Prince Phillip and recently that of Prince William and Kate Middleton.

Breakfast at Duck and Waffle

Saturday Afternoon

Shopping and Sightseeing in London

Big Ben is the first place I visited.Big Ben is a tower clock known for its accuracy and for its massive hour bell. Strictly speaking, the name refers only to the bell, which weighs 15.1 tons (13.7 metric tons), but it is commonly associated with the whole clock tower at the northern end of the Houses of Parliament, in the London borough of Westminster.Lastly, I went to Oxford Street which is a popular shopping street in the West End of London. It is one of London's best-known streets, containing a plethora of famous department stores as well as the high-street shops.

Saturday Evening

Dining at the 135

Dining at 135, is a three-course meal on the London Eye.A 360 degree rotation lasts for 30 minutes and as the first begins, starters are served whilst taking in the view.

Sunday Morning

Sunday Breakfast at Andina

Andina is a Peruvian restaurant.Shares a variety of dishes as is the Peruvian way including pork belly bites, tuna ceviche and corn cakes with poached eggs.

Sunday Afternoon

Sightseeing on Double Decker Buses

London's iconic double-decker buses are a convenient and cheap way to travel around the city, with plenty of sightseeing opportunities along the way.
